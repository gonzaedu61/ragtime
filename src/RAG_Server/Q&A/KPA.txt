KPA steht für „Kontrakt, Produktion und Abruf“.  
Es bezeichnet eine Verwaltungsfunktion, mit der in der Artikelpositions-Maske (P-Maske) Vertrags-, Produktions- und Abrufaufträge miteinander verknüpft und abgewickelt werden.
(ragtime) PS C:\dev\ragtime> rag "When should I use a KPA approach for the sales order?" pepe.json
You should only switch on the KPA (Contract–Production–Call-off) logic for a sales order when you really need to break one “big” order into legally or operationally distinct steps – customer contract, separate production order(s) and one or more delivery (call-off) orders – and still keep all of those individual documents linked together. 

In practice that means:

• Your order cannot (or must not) be delivered in one go  
• You want to generate distinct manufacturing and delivery orders under a single umbrella contract  
• You need to analyse or report “contract vs. production vs. call-off” quantities and values as one consolidated view  

If none of those apply, a standard sales order without KPA is sufficient.
