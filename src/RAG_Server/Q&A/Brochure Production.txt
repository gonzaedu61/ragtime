ALMENDRO supports a brochure production end-to-end by integrating the sales order, procurement, shop‐floor planning and production tracking in one system.  In outline:

1. Print list as procurement basis  
   • From the brochure’s technical data (format, paper grades, weights, run lengths) ALMENDRO produces a “Druckliste” that shows material requirements (e.g. 250 g glossy, 80 g matt) with dates and quantities.  
   • Purchasing can then reserve stock in‐system or convert requirements directly into purchase orders.

2. Purchase‐order management  
   • You can attach up to four different text types and addresses to each purchase‐order position (e.g. delivery instructions, special notes).  
   • Standard ALMENDRO text‐editor functions are available.  
   • Supplier confirmations (“Bestätigung”) can be uploaded centrally or per line, then compared automatically against the original order and differences posted.

3. Material planning and reservation  
   • A dedicated “Materialplanung (offen>0)” screen lists all open requirements by paper‐item, showing what is needed, reserved and still free.  
   • From there purchasing triggers reservations in the order (“Reservieren im Auftrag”) or places new orders (“Bestellung”) with a single click.

4. Generation of shop‐floor work plans  
   • Based on the brochure’s technical description ALMENDRO automatically generates the entries in the production mask (“F-Maske”).  
   • These work plans carry the cost‐center codes and operation numbers needed for scheduling, capacity planning and performance recording.

5. Finished‐goods and inventory posting  
   • When the brochures are completed, ALMENDRO can automatically post them as finished‐goods stock.  
   • You can configure which quantities (total, delivered, already billed, internally produced, etc.) are proposed when converting order lines into stock‐posting lines.

By linking sales orders, material requirements, purchasing, supplier confirmation, work‐plan generation and stock‐posting, ALMENDRO ensures that all paper and process steps for brochure production are planned, ordered and executed in a single, transparent workflow.
